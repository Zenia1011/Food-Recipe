Sambar




  • INGREDIENTS:

  • 1/3 cup toor dal
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 cup chopped mixed vegetables
  • 1/2 tsp mustard
  • 6 curry leaves
  • 2 dry red chillies
  • pinch of asofoetida
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 1 chopped tomato
  • 1 cup soya chunk
  • 1 tbsp sambar masala powder
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 cup oil + 1 & 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp chopped corriander
  • salt as per taste



  • METHOD: 
    •          Tamarind Water
          
          Soak 1/2 tbsp tamarind in 3 tbsp hot water for 10-15 mins.
          
          Mash the soaked tamarind with a spoon or by hand & filter the water using a strainer.
          
          Add toor dal, turmeric powder & 1 cup water in a 4 litre capacity steel or aluminium pressure cooker.
          
          Put mixed vegetables in a small. 
          
          Close the lid of the cooker & cook medium flame for 3-4 whistles.
          
          In this recipe we have used 1 carrot, brinjal and french beans chopped in cube.
          
          Turn off the gas & let the cooker cool down.
          
          When the pressure of the cooker is released, open its lid & take out the vegetable container.
          
          Mash the dal using a spoon or blender.

         
          Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan on medium flame.
         
          Add mustard seeds.
         
          When mustard seeds start spluttering, add curry leaves, dry red chilly and asofoetida.
         
          Fry 10-15 secs.
         
          Add chopped onion.
         
          Fry the onion until it turns light pink.
         
          Add tamarind water water and cook for 2-3 mins.
         
          Add chopped tomatoes.
         
          Fry the tomatoes till they turn soft.
         
          Add sambhar powder.
         
          Fry for a minute while stirring with a spoon.
         
          Add boiled and mashed dal, boiled vegetables, soya chunks 1 & 1/2 cups water & salt.
         
         
          Mix well & let it boil.
         
          Lert it boil for about 7 mins.
         
          Turn off the gas.
         
          Take out the sambar in a bowl & garnish with finely chopped corriander.
